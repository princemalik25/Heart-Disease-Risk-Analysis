# Heart Disease Risk Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project uses **Power BI** integrated with **SQL, Python, and Excel** to analyze health datasets and uncover risk factors associated with **heart disease**.  
The dashboard is designed with multiple sheets to provide a **comprehensive, multi-angle view** of patient health indicators and their correlations.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Identify **key risk factors** contributing to heart disease.
- Compare health indicators across **age categories** (Young, Middle-Aged, Senior).
- Highlight **silent risks** (asymptomatic patients) that often go undetected.
- Provide **actionable insights** for healthcare professionals, researchers, and stakeholders.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Structure
The dashboard consists of **five analytical sheets**:

1. **Overview**
   - Population metrics (total patients, average cholesterol, heart rate, blood pressure).
   - Gender and age group segmentation.

2. **BP Analysis**
   - Distribution of blood pressure categories (High, Medium, Normal).
   - Correlation with blood sugar, cholesterol, and chest pain.

3. **Cholesterol Analysis**
   - Breakdown of cholesterol levels (Normal, Borderline, High).
   - Relationship with chest pain types and blood sugar levels.

4. **Blood Sugar Analysis**
   - Normal vs high sugar distribution.
   - Impact on cholesterol, blood pressure, and chest pain categories.

5. **Chest Pain Analysis**
   - Distribution across asymptomatic, non-anginal, atypical angina, and typical angina.
   - Correlation with cholesterol, ECG results, and blood pressure.

---

## 🔑 Key Insights
- **Silent risk dominates**: Over 50% of patients are asymptomatic, yet many show high cholesterol, high BP, or abnormal ECG results.
- **Hypertension is pervasive**: High blood pressure is the most consistent risk factor across all categories.
- **Blood sugar amplifies risk**: Patients with high sugar are disproportionately in the high cholesterol group.
- **ECG abnormalities matter**: Non-anginal patients often show serious heart conditions despite lack of symptoms.
